What is Equity Fund ?


An equity fund is a popular scheme in mutual funds. Equity fund allows the investors to invest directly in the stock market which helps in getting huge returns. Despite giving huge returns it is categorized as a high-risk fund as it is directly affected by the stock market.

An equity fund is good for those who want to gain huge returns for both the long term and the short term. The risk of investment must be kept in mind before investing.


What are the types of Equity Fund?

There are mainly three types of Equity Fund,


Sector-specific Fund

The sector-specific fund invests in stocks but in a particular sector of the stock market. This helps the investors to find a particular growing sector and invest in it to gain more profit, but there is a huge risk of loss if the particular sector falls. It is advisable to diversify the portfolio in different sectors.

Example: IT-Sector and Automobile-Sector.


Index Fund

Index funds track the index of the stock market like Nifty or Sensex. The profit depends on the market index performance. Index funds have a low Expanse Ratio which is great for long-term investors.


Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS)

Equity Linked Savings Scheme allows the investors to save tax. They invest in equities to gain huge returns. This type of fund usually has a lock-in period for specific years. The investors don't have to pay tax for this scheme.
